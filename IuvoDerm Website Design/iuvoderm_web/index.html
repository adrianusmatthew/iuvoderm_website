What is iuvoderm?

In 2017, 425 million adults worldwide suffer from diabetes, with the numbers predicted to rise to 629 million by 2045. About 80% of people with diabetes suffer from diabetic neuropathy, a condition which causes nerve damage in lower limbs, reducing a diabetic’s awareness to injuries on their foot. Left untreated, these injuries may get infected and develop into a foot ulcer, which if further left untreated, ultimately leads to amputation. 1 in every 4 diabetics will develop a foot ulcer at some point in their lives, and a further 1 in every 4 who undergo the procedure die within 30 days, reaching up to 80% mortality rate in 5 years post-surgery, showing the frailty of the diabetic population.


To tackle this problem, we formulated iuvoderm, a smart wound dressing designed to help foot ulcer caregivers (i.e doctors to nurses) manage their patient’s treatments better and smarter with data driven decisions. Our dressing, while designed to improve healing of the ulcer, is also packed with sensors for monitoring the wound’s condition in real time, providing caregivers with diagnostics data that’ll help them make more informed medical decisions on how to proceed with their patient’s wound care, reducing the diabetic patient's risk of amputation.
